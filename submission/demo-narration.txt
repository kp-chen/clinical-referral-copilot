Referral triage is high stakes. Clinical Referral Copilot offers a safer pattern: AI structures; clinicians decide.

Using only synthetic gastroenterology referrals, a clinician reviews the narrative and asks for a structured urgency recommendation. The server-side key never reaches the browser.

GPT five point six uses the OpenAI Responses API with strict Structured Outputs and no storage. It returns urgency, confidence, facts, missing information, and reasons. Selecting a reason highlights the exact source phrase and its synthetic rule.

The model cannot take clinical action. A clinician approves, edits, or rejects, while the audit records that decision without storing the referral narrative.

The evaluation view separates evidence from persuasion. This tiny synthetic gold set is explicitly illustrative: four urgency labels agree, while one disagreement remains visible for review.

Identifiers and high-confidence prompt-injection patterns are blocked before any model call. Evidence appears only when every quoted phrase exists in the supplied narrative.

This is not a medical device and makes no clinical-performance claim. It is a Build Week prototype of transparent, clinician-in-the-loop reasoning: synthetic data, inspectable evidence, bounded agency, and human authority.
